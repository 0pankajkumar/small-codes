<p><b>Rahul Gandhi</b> <span class="unicode haudio"><span class="fn"><span style="white-space:nowrap"><a href="/wiki/File:Rahul_Rajiv_Gandhi.ogg" title="About this sound"><img alt="About this sound" data-file-height="20" data-file-width="20" height="11" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/11px-Loudspeaker.svg.png" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/17px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Loudspeaker.svg/22px-Loudspeaker.svg.png 2x" width="11"/></a> </span><a class="internal" href="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/f/ff/Rahul_Rajiv_Gandhi.ogg" title="Rahul Rajiv Gandhi.ogg">pronunciation</a></span> <small class="metadata audiolinkinfo" style="cursor:help;">(<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Media_help" title="Wikipedia:Media help"><span style="cursor:help;">help</span></a>·<a href="/wiki/File:Rahul_Rajiv_Gandhi.ogg" title="File:Rahul Rajiv Gandhi.ogg"><span style="cursor:help;">info</span></a>)</small></span> <span class="IPA" title="Representation in the International Phonetic Alphabet (IPA)"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/Hindi_and_Urdu" title="Help:IPA/Hindi and Urdu">[ˈraːɦʊl ˈɡaːnd̪ʱiː]</a></span> (born 19 June 1970) is an Indian politician. He is the President of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Congress" title="Indian National Congress">Indian National Congress</a> and a member of the <a href="/wiki/16th_Lok_Sabha" title="16th Lok Sabha">16th Lok Sabha</a> from <a href="/wiki/Amethi_(Lok_Sabha_constituency)" title="Amethi (Lok Sabha constituency)">Amethi</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> In addition to that, Gandhi serves as the Chairperson of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Youth_Congress" title="Indian Youth Congress">Indian Youth Congress</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Students_Union_of_India" title="National Students Union of India">National Students Union of India</a>.</p>
<p>Born to <a href="/wiki/Sonia_Gandhi" title="Sonia Gandhi">Sonia</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rajiv_Gandhi" title="Rajiv Gandhi">Rajiv Gandhi</a> of the <a href="/wiki/Nehru%E2%80%93Gandhi_family" title="Nehru–Gandhi family">Nehru–Gandhi family</a>, Rahul shifted school in his youth because of security concerns. He studied abroad under a pseudonym, his identity being known only to a select few including university officials and security agencies. After obtaining degrees in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/International_Relations" title="International Relations">International Relations</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Development_Studies" title="Development Studies">Development Studies</a> at the universities of <a href="/wiki/Rollins_College" title="Rollins College">Rollins</a> and <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge" title="Trinity College, Cambridge">Cambridge</a>, Gandhi worked at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monitor_Group" title="Monitor Group">Monitor Group</a>, a management consulting firm in London, before establishing the <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>-based technology outsourcing firm, Backops Services Private Ltd.</p>
<p>Gandhi entered politics in 2004, having stayed away from <a href="/wiki/Public_sphere" title="Public sphere">public sphere</a> prior to that. He successfully contested the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_general_elections,_2004" title="Indian general elections, 2004">Indian general elections, 2004</a> from Amethi, a seat that was earlier held by his father; he won again from the constituency in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_general_elections,_2009" title="Indian general elections, 2009">2009</a> and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_general_elections,_2014" title="Indian general elections, 2014">2014</a>. Amidst calls from Congress party veterans for his greater involvement in party politics and national government, Gandhi was elected Congress Vice-President in 2013, having served as the General Secretary previously. Gandhi led <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Congress_campaign_for_Indian_general_election,_2014" title="Indian National Congress campaign for Indian general election, 2014">Congress' campaign</a> in the 2014 Indian general elections; the party suffered its worst electoral result in its history, winning only 44 seats compared to 206 seats won previously in the 2009 general election.</p>
<p>Gandhi took over as the president of the Congress in December 2017. He is also a <a href="/wiki/Trustee" title="Trustee">Trustee</a> of <a href="/wiki/Rajiv_Gandhi_Foundation" title="Rajiv Gandhi Foundation">Rajiv Gandhi Foundation</a> and <a href="/wiki/Rajiv_Gandhi_Charitable_Trust" title="Rajiv Gandhi Charitable Trust">Rajiv Gandhi Charitable Trust</a>.</p>

<p>Gandhi was born in <a href="/wiki/Delhi" title="Delhi">Delhi</a> on 19 June 1970,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-3"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup> as the first of the two children of <a href="/wiki/Rajiv_Gandhi" title="Rajiv Gandhi">Rajiv Gandhi</a>, who later became the <a href="/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_India" title="Prime Minister of India">Prime Minister of India</a>, and Italian-born <a href="/wiki/Sonia_Gandhi" title="Sonia Gandhi">Sonia Gandhi</a>, who later became President of <a href="/wiki/Indian_National_Congress" title="Indian National Congress">Indian National Congress</a>, and as the grandson of the then Prime Minister <a href="/wiki/Indira_Gandhi" title="Indira Gandhi">Indira Gandhi</a>. His paternal grandfather <a href="/wiki/Feroze_Gandhi" title="Feroze Gandhi">Feroze Gandhi</a> was a <a href="/wiki/Parsi" title="Parsi">Parsi</a> from <a href="/wiki/Gujarat" title="Gujarat">Gujarat</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-4"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup> He is also the great-grandson of India's first Prime Minister, <a href="/wiki/Jawaharlal_Nehru" title="Jawaharlal Nehru">Jawaharlal Nehru</a>. <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Priyanka_Vadra" title="Priyanka Vadra">Priyanka Vadra</a> is his younger sister<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-5"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Robert_Vadra" title="Robert Vadra">Robert Vadra</a> is his brother-in-law. He identifies himself as a Hindu Brahmin.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-6"><a href="#cite_note-6">[6]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gandhi attended <a href="/wiki/St._Columba%27s_School,_Delhi" title="St. Columba's School, Delhi">St. Columba's School, Delhi</a><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-7"><a href="#cite_note-7">[7]</a></sup> before entering <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Doon_school" title="Doon school">The Doon School</a> in <a href="/wiki/Dehradun" title="Dehradun">Dehradun</a>, <a href="/wiki/Uttarakhand" title="Uttarakhand">Uttarakhand</a> from 1981 to 1983. Meanwhile, his father had joined politics and became the Prime Minister on 31 October 1984 when Indira Gandhi was <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Indira_Gandhi" title="Assassination of Indira Gandhi">assassinated</a>. Due to the security threats faced by Indira Gandhi's family from <a href="/wiki/Punjab_insurgency" title="Punjab insurgency">Sikh extremists</a>, Rahul Gandhi and his sister, Priyanka were home-schooled thereafter.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-NYTimes_8-0"><a href="#cite_note-NYTimes-8">[8]</a></sup> Rahul Gandhi joined <a href="/wiki/St._Stephen%27s_College,_Delhi" title="St. Stephen's College, Delhi">St. Stephen's College, Delhi</a> in 1989 for his undergraduate education but moved to <a href="/wiki/Harvard_University" title="Harvard University">Harvard University</a> after he completed the first year examinations.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dnaed_9-0"><a href="#cite_note-dnaed-9">[9]</a></sup> In 1991, after Rajiv Gandhi was <a href="/wiki/Assassination_of_Rajiv_Gandhi" title="Assassination of Rajiv Gandhi">assassinated</a> by the Tamil Tigers (<a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/LTTE" title="LTTE">LTTE</a>)<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-10"><a href="#cite_note-10">[10]</a></sup> during an election rally, he shifted to <a href="/wiki/Rollins_College" title="Rollins College">Rollins College</a> in Florida, USA, due to security concerns and obtained his B.A. in 1994.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-11"><a href="#cite_note-11">[11]</a></sup> During his time at Rollins, he assumed the pseudonym Raul Vinci and his identity was known only to the university officials and security agencies.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dnaed_9-1"><a href="#cite_note-dnaed-9">[9]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-12"><a href="#cite_note-12">[12]</a></sup> He further went on to obtain an M.Phil from <a href="/wiki/Trinity_College,_Cambridge" title="Trinity College, Cambridge">Trinity College, Cambridge</a> in 1995.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-13"><a href="#cite_note-13">[13]</a></sup> After graduation, Rahul Gandhi worked at the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Monitor_Group" title="Monitor Group">Monitor Group</a>, a <a href="/wiki/Management_consulting" title="Management consulting">management consulting</a> firm, in London.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-14"><a href="#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup> In 2002, he was one of the directors of <a href="/wiki/Mumbai" title="Mumbai">Mumbai</a>-based <a href="/wiki/Outsourcing" title="Outsourcing">technology outsourcing</a> firm Backops Services Private Ltd.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-15"><a href="#cite_note-15">[15]</a></sup> While in 2004, Gandhi had told the press that he had a Spanish girlfriend, an architect, living in Venezuela, whom he had met while studying in England.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-16"><a href="#cite_note-16">[16]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-17"><a href="#cite_note-17">[17]</a></sup> In 2013, he suggested that he may not get married.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-18"><a href="#cite_note-18">[18]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Formative_years">Formative years</span></h2>
<p>In March 2004, Gandhi announced his entry into politics by announcing that he would contest the May 2004 elections, standing for his father's former constituency of <a href="/wiki/Amethi" title="Amethi">Amethi</a> in Uttar Pradesh in the <a href="/wiki/Lok_Sabha" title="Lok Sabha">Lok Sabha</a>, India's lower house of Parliament.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amethi_19-0"><a href="#cite_note-amethi-19">[19]</a></sup> The seat had been held by his mother until she transferred to the neighbouring seat of <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Rae_Bareilly" title="Rae Bareilly">Rae Bareilly</a>. The Congress had been doing poorly in Uttar Pradesh, holding only 10 of the 80 Lok Sabha seats in the state at the time.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amethihistory_20-0"><a href="#cite_note-amethihistory-20">[20]</a></sup> At the time, this move generated surprise among political commentators, who had regarded his sister <a href="/wiki/Priyanka_Gandhi" title="Priyanka Gandhi">Priyanka</a> as being the more charismatic and likely to succeed. It generated speculation that the presence of a young member of India's most famous political family would reinvigorate the Congress party's political fortunes among India's youthful population<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-rahulrun_21-0"><a href="#cite_note-rahulrun-21">[21]</a></sup> In his first interview with foreign media, Gandhi portrayed himself as a uniter of the country and condemned "divisive" politics in India, saying that he would try to reduce <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Indian_caste_system" title="Indian caste system">caste</a> and <a href="/wiki/Religion_in_India" title="Religion in India">religious</a> tensions.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-amethi_19-1"><a href="#cite_note-amethi-19">[19]</a></sup> Gandhi won, retaining the family stronghold with a victory margin of over 100,000.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-22"><a href="#cite_note-22">[22]</a></sup> Until 2006 he held no other office.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ttelegraph_23-0"><a href="#cite_note-ttelegraph-23">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gandhi and his sister, Priyanka managed their mother's campaign for re-election to Rae Bareilly in 2006, which was won with a victory margin of over 400,000 votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-24"><a href="#cite_note-24">[24]</a></sup> He was a prominent figure in the Congress campaign for the 2007 Uttar Pradesh Assembly elections; Congress, however, won only 22 seats of the 403 seats with 8.53% of votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-25"><a href="#cite_note-25">[25]</a></sup></p>
<p>Gandhi was appointed General Secretary of the <a href="/wiki/All_India_Congress_Committee" title="All India Congress Committee">All India Congress Committee</a> on 24 September 2007 in a reshuffle of the party secretariat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-26"><a href="#cite_note-26">[26]</a></sup> In the same reshuffle, he was also given charge of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Youth_Congress" title="Indian Youth Congress">Indian Youth Congress</a> and the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/National_Students_Union_of_India" title="National Students Union of India">National Students Union of India</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-27"><a href="#cite_note-27">[27]</a></sup> In 2008, senior Congress leader Veerappa Moily mentioned "Rahul-as-PM" idea when the PM of India Manmohan Singh was still abroad.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-28"><a href="#cite_note-28">[28]</a></sup> He was elevated to the position of the vice-president of the party in January 2013.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-29"><a href="#cite_note-29">[29]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Youth_politics">Youth politics</span></h3>
<p>In September 2007 when he was appointed general secretary in charge of the <a href="/wiki/Indian_Youth_Congress" title="Indian Youth Congress">Indian Youth Congress</a> (IYC) and the National Students Union of India (NSUI), Gandhi promised to reform youth politics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-30"><a href="#cite_note-30">[30]</a></sup> In his attempt to prove himself thus, in November 2008 Gandhi held interviews at his 12 Tughlak Lane residence in New Delhi to handpick at least 40 people who will make up the think-tank of the Indian Youth Congress (IYC), an organisation that he has been keen to transform since he was appointed general secretary in September 2007.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-31"><a href="#cite_note-31">[31]</a></sup></p>
<p>Under Gandhi, the IYC and NSUI have seen a dramatic increase in members from 200,000 to 2.5 million.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-32"><a href="#cite_note-32">[32]</a></sup> <i>The Indian Express</i> wrote in 2011, "Three years later, as another organisational reshuffle is in the offing, Gandhi's dream remains unrealised with party veterans manipulating internal elections in the Youth Congress and a host of people with questionable background gaining entry into it."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Express2_33-0"><a href="#cite_note-Express2-33">[33]</a></sup></p>

<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/2009_Lok_Sabha_elections" title="2009 Lok Sabha elections">2009 Lok Sabha elections</a>, Gandhi retained his Amethi seat by defeating his nearest rival by a margin of over 370,000 votes. Gandhi was credited with the Congress revival in Uttar Pradesh where they won 21 out of the total 80 Lok Sabha seats.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-34"><a href="#cite_note-34">[34]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009election_35-0"><a href="#cite_note-2009election-35">[35]</a></sup> He spoke at 125 rallies across the country in six weeks.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-36"><a href="#cite_note-36">[36]</a></sup> The nationwide elections defied the predictions made by pre-poll predictions and exit polls and gave a clear mandate to the incumbent Congress-led UPA government.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-37"><a href="#cite_note-37">[37]</a></sup></p>
<p>In May 2011, Gandhi was arrested by the <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh_Police" title="Uttar Pradesh Police">Uttar Pradesh Police</a> at Bhatta Parsaul village after he turned out in support of <a href="/wiki/2011_land_acquisition_protests_in_Uttar_Pradesh" title="2011 land acquisition protests in Uttar Pradesh">agitating farmers</a> demanding more compensation for their land being acquired for a highway project. Gandhi was taken away from the protest site and later given bail and dropped off at the Delhi-UP border.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-38"><a href="#cite_note-38">[38]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2012_Assembly_elections">2012 Assembly elections</span></h2>
<p>Gandhi campaigned during the <a href="/wiki/2012_elections_in_India" title="2012 elections in India">2012 Assembly elections</a> in the politically crucial <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh_legislative_assembly_election,_2012" title="Uttar Pradesh legislative assembly election, 2012">Uttar Pradesh election</a> for almost two months, holding 200 rallies. However Congress ranked as the fourth party in the state, winning 28 seats, an increase of six seats from the previous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh_legislative_assembly_election,_2007" title="Uttar Pradesh legislative assembly election, 2007">2007 elections</a>. Out of the 15 seats in the Amethi parliamentary constituency, Congress won two.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-39"><a href="#cite_note-39">[39]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-IT-1_40-0"><a href="#cite_note-IT-1-40">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Congress activists defended the result in Uttar Pradesh, saying "there's a big difference between state elections and national polls and in the end there is only the first family, a hope and a prayer",<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-41"><a href="#cite_note-41">[41]</a></sup> and pointing out the turn around attributed to Gandhi in the 2009 Lok Sabha national elections in the state. However, Rahul Gandhi publicly accepted responsibility for the result in an interview after the result was declared.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-TO_42-0"><a href="#cite_note-TO-42">[42]</a></sup></p>
<p>In the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gujarat_legislative_assembly_election,_2012" title="Gujarat legislative assembly election, 2012">Gujarat assembly elections</a> held later in the year, Gandhi was not made the head of the election campaign. This was seen and regarded by opponents as an admission of defeat and was termed as a tactic to avoid blame of defeat.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-ET-1_43-0"><a href="#cite_note-ET-1-43">[43]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-44"><a href="#cite_note-44">[44]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-45"><a href="#cite_note-45">[45]</a></sup> Congress won 57 seats in the assembly of 182, which was 2 less than the previous <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Gujarat_legislative_assembly_election,_2007" title="Gujarat legislative assembly election, 2007">elections in 2007</a>. Later in bypolls, Congress lost 4 more seats to BJP.</p>
<h2><span id="Indian_general_election.2C_2014"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Indian_general_election,_2014">Indian general election, 2014</span></h2>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Indian_general_election,_2014" title="Indian general election, 2014">Indian general election, 2014</a></div>
<p>Gandhi contested the <a href="/wiki/Indian_general_election,_2014" title="Indian general election, 2014">Indian general election, 2014</a> from his constituency, Amethi.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-46"><a href="#cite_note-46">[46]</a></sup> and led the election campaign of the Indian National Congress.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-R1_47-0"><a href="#cite_note-R1-47">[47]</a></sup> Gandhi held the Amethi seat by defeating his nearest rival, BJP's <a href="/wiki/Smriti_Irani" title="Smriti Irani">Smriti Irani</a>, by a reduced margin of 107,000 votes.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009election_35-1"><a href="#cite_note-2009election-35">[35]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-48"><a href="#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> Under his leadership, the Indian National Congress suffered its worst ever performance in elections and won only 44 seats compared to 206 seats won previously in the 2009 general election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-dna_May_2014_49-0"><a href="#cite_note-dna_May_2014-49">[49]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009results_50-0"><a href="#cite_note-2009results-50">[50]</a></sup> The Congress-led electoral alliance, the UPA also had its worst ever performance in elections and won only 59 seats compared to 262 seats won previously in the 2009 general election.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-2009results_50-1"><a href="#cite_note-2009results-50">[50]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-51"><a href="#cite_note-51">[51]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-52"><a href="#cite_note-52">[52]</a></sup> After the defeat, Gandhi offered to resign his posts, only to be rejected by the party's working committee.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-53"><a href="#cite_note-53">[53]</a></sup></p>

<p>In February 2015, Gandhi went on a leave of absence to an "undisclosed location to reflect both on recent events related to the party and its future course".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-54"><a href="#cite_note-54">[54]</a></sup> After returning from his leave of absence, Gandhi addressed the farmer and worker's rally, named as <i>Kisan Khet Mazdoor</i> Rally in <a href="/wiki/Ramlila_Maidan" title="Ramlila Maidan">Ramlila Maidan</a> on 19 April 2015. Here he made "references to his agitations in <a href="/wiki/Niyamgiri" title="Niyamgiri">Niyamgiri</a> in <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Orissa,_India" title="Orissa, India">Orissa</a> and Bhatta-Parsaul in Greater Noida in <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a>".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KKM_55-0"><a href="#cite_note-KKM-55">[55]</a></sup> The rally was attended by 1 lakh people. In the speech he gave, he criticised the Indian prime minister <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Narendra Modi</a> about his comment in <a href="/wiki/Toronto" title="Toronto">Toronto</a> where he said that he was "cleaning the mess created by previous governments".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-56"><a href="#cite_note-56">[56]</a></sup> He also said "Do you know how <a href="/wiki/Narendra_Modi" title="Narendra Modi">Modi</a> won the election?.. He borrowed thousands of crores from industrialists for his several campaigns and advertisements. How will he pay them back? He will pay them back with your land. He will give your land to his industrialist friends."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-KKM_55-1"><a href="#cite_note-KKM-55">[55]</a></sup></p>
<p>He mocked the government as "suit-boot government", a reference to Modi's monogrammed suit which he wore in the Republic Day meeting with <a href="/wiki/Barack_Obama" title="Barack Obama">Barack Obama</a>. Moreover, he used the "acche din government" jibe (which was Modi's election campaign slogan meaning "good days government") and mentioned that it had "failed the country".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-57"><a href="#cite_note-57">[57]</a></sup></p>
<p>A land bill was introduced by the BJP government in the parliament in May which was criticised by the opposition parties.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LB_58-0"><a href="#cite_note-LB-58">[58]</a></sup> Accusing the government of "murdering" <a href="/wiki/United_Progressive_Alliance" title="United Progressive Alliance">UPA</a>'s land bill,<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-59"><a href="#cite_note-59">[59]</a></sup> Gandhi promised to prevent the bill from being passed, if not in the parliament then would "stop you [ <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BJP" title="BJP">BJP</a> government] on streets". He further accused the government of diluting the bill and called it as "anti-farmer".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-LB_58-1"><a href="#cite_note-LB-58">[58]</a></sup> Gandhi also drew parallel between "daylight robbery" and the bill.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-60"><a href="#cite_note-60">[60]</a></sup> On 26 May, the day of the Modi government's first anniversary, Gandhi commented at a rally in <a href="/wiki/Kozhikode" title="Kozhikode">Kozhikode</a> "<i>Unfortunately, birthday celebrations is only for a few powerful friends of the government. Kisan, farmers and mazdoor have nothing to celebrate.</i>" <sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-61"><a href="#cite_note-61">[61]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Political_and_social_views">Political and social views</span></h2>

<p>In December 2010 during the <a href="/wiki/United_States_diplomatic_cables_leak" title="United States diplomatic cables leak">United States diplomatic cables leak</a>, <a href="/wiki/WikiLeaks" title="WikiLeaks">WikiLeaks</a> leaked a cable dated 3 August 2009, where the Prime Minister of India, <a href="/wiki/Manmohan_Singh" title="Manmohan Singh">Manmohan Singh</a> had hosted a lunch on 20 July 2009 for Rahul Gandhi, then the General Secretary of the AICC. One of the guests who was invited for the lunch was the <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/United_States_Ambassador_to_India" title="United States Ambassador to India">United States Ambassador to India</a>, <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Timothy_J._Roemer" title="Timothy J. Roemer">Timothy J. Roemer</a>. In a "candid conversation" with Roemer, he said that he believes <a href="/wiki/Hindu" title="Hindu">Hindu</a> <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Extremists" title="Extremists">extremists</a> pose a greater threat to his country than <a href="/wiki/Muslim" title="Muslim">Muslim</a> militants. Rahul Gandhi referred specifically to more-polarising figures in the <a href="/wiki/Bharatiya_Janata_Party" title="Bharatiya Janata Party">Bharatiya Janata Party</a>. Also responding to the ambassador's query about the activities in the region by the <a href="/wiki/Islam" title="Islam">Islamist</a> militant organisation <a href="/wiki/Lashkar-e-Taiba" title="Lashkar-e-Taiba">Lashkar-e-Taiba</a> (LeT), Rahul Gandhi said there was evidence of some support for the group among certain elements in India's indigenous <a href="/wiki/Islam_in_India" title="Islam in India">Muslim</a> population.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-62"><a href="#cite_note-62">[62]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-63"><a href="#cite_note-63">[63]</a></sup> In a response to this, the BJP heavily criticised Rahul Gandhi for his statements. BJP spokesperson <a href="/wiki/Ravi_Shankar_Prasad" title="Ravi Shankar Prasad">Ravi Shankar Prasad</a> slammed Rahul Gandhi, saying that his language was a bigger threat to India, dividing the people of the country on communal grounds. Speaking to reporters, Prasad said, "In one stroke Mr. Rahul Gandhi has sought to give a big leverage to the propaganda to all the extremist and terrorist groups in <a href="/wiki/Pakistan" title="Pakistan">Pakistan</a>, and also some segments of Pakistani establishment. It would also seriously compromise India's fight against terror as also our strategic security." Adding that terrorism has no religion, he said that Rahul Gandhi had shown his lack of understanding India.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-64"><a href="#cite_note-64">[64]</a></sup> Gandhi has also been critical of groups like the <a href="/wiki/Rashtriya_Swayamsevak_Sangh" title="Rashtriya Swayamsevak Sangh">RSS</a> and has compared them to terrorist organisations like <a href="/wiki/Students_Islamic_Movement_of_India" title="Students Islamic Movement of India">SIMI</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-Isfanatical_65-0"><a href="#cite_note-Isfanatical-65">[65]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-66"><a href="#cite_note-66">[66]</a></sup></p>
<p>After the <a href="/wiki/2013_Muzaffarnagar_riots" title="2013 Muzaffarnagar riots">2013 Muzaffarnagar riots</a>, at a <a href="/wiki/Madhya_Pradesh_Legislative_Assembly_election,_2013" title="Madhya Pradesh Legislative Assembly election, 2013">Madhya Pradesh election rally</a> in Indore, Rahul Gandhi claimed that a police officer has told him that Pakistan's <a href="/wiki/Inter-Services_Intelligence" title="Inter-Services Intelligence">ISI</a> was trying to recruit disgruntled riot-affected youngsters.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-67"><a href="#cite_note-67">[67]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-68"><a href="#cite_note-68">[68]</a></sup> However, the district administration, the UP state government, the Union Home Ministry, the Research and Analysis Wing (RAW) and the Intelligence Bureau (IB) denied any such development.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-69"><a href="#cite_note-69">[69]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-70"><a href="#cite_note-70">[70]</a></sup> This remark drew heavy criticism from various political outfits such as BJP, SP, CPI and JD(U).<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-71"><a href="#cite_note-71">[71]</a></sup> Congress's <a href="/wiki/Jairam_Ramesh" title="Jairam Ramesh">Jairam Ramesh</a> said Gandhi needed to apologise to the Muslim community for this remark.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-72"><a href="#cite_note-72">[72]</a></sup> In reply to the <a href="/wiki/Election_Commission_of_India" title="Election Commission of India">ECI's</a> show-cause notice to explain why action should not be initiated against him for violating the Model Code of Conduct, Gandhi said that he didn't intend to exploit communal sentiments but was referring to divisive politics.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-73"><a href="#cite_note-73">[73]</a></sup> The BJP also asked the government to explain why Gandhi, who holds no post in the government, is being briefed by intelligence officers on important security issues.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-74"><a href="#cite_note-74">[74]</a></sup> On 13 November 2013, Election Commission of India finding Gandhi's explanation to be insufficient conveyed its displeasure and advised him to be more circumspect in his public utterances during election campaigns.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-75"><a href="#cite_note-75">[75]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Lokpal">Lokpal</span></h3>
<p>Rahul Gandhi opines that the <a href="/wiki/Lokpal" title="Lokpal">Lokpal</a> should be made a constitutional body and it should be made accountable to the Parliament, just like the Election Commission of India. He also feels that Lokpal alone cannot root out corruption. This statement came out on 25 August 2011, on the 10th day of <a href="/wiki/Anna_Hazare" title="Anna Hazare">Anna Hazare</a>'s fast. This statement was considered as a delaying tactic by the opposition and <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Team_Anna" title="Team Anna">Team Anna</a>'s members. It was consequently slammed by prominent opposition leaders <a href="/wiki/Sushma_Swaraj" title="Sushma Swaraj">Sushma Swaraj</a> and <a href="/wiki/Arun_Jaitley" title="Arun Jaitley">Arun Jaitley</a>.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-76"><a href="#cite_note-76">[76]</a></sup> The Parliamentary Standing Committee led by Abhishek Manu Singhvi tabled the <a href="/wiki/Jan_Lokpal_Bill" title="Jan Lokpal Bill">Jan Lokpal Bill</a> report in the Rajya Sabha on 9 December 2011. The report recommended the Lokpal to be made into a constitutional body. In response, Hazare attacked Rahul Gandhi, claiming he had made the bill "weak and ineffective".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-77"><a href="#cite_note-77">[77]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Poverty">Poverty</span></h3>

Rahul Gandhi speaking at The Doon School's Model United Nations in 2017</div>

<p>During a rally in <a href="/wiki/Allahabad" title="Allahabad">Allahabad</a>, Gandhi said "Poverty is just a state of mind. It does not mean the scarcity of food, money or material things. If one possesses self-confidence, then one can overcome poverty."<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-78"><a href="#cite_note-78">[78]</a></sup> The phrase "state of mind" drew sharp criticism with <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/BJP" title="BJP">BJP</a> <a href="/wiki/Uttar_Pradesh" title="Uttar Pradesh">Uttar Pradesh</a> spokesperson Vijay Bahadur Pathak alleging him to be mocking the poor.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-79"><a href="#cite_note-79">[79]</a></sup> <i><a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Oneindia_News" title="Oneindia News">Oneindia News</a></i> wrote that he made "economics irrelevant".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-80"><a href="#cite_note-80">[80]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Ordinance_on_Convicted_Lawmakers">Ordinance on Convicted Lawmakers</span></h3>
<div class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable" role="note">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Disqualification_of_convicted_representatives_in_India" title="Disqualification of convicted representatives in India">Disqualification of convicted representatives in India</a></div>
<p>Rahul Gandhi, on "the convicted lawmakers ordinance" insisted that the ordinance is "complete nonsense"<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-81"><a href="#cite_note-81">[81]</a></sup> and "What our government has done is wrong".<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-82"><a href="#cite_note-82">[82]</a></sup> This was expressed on 28 September 2013 in a press meet, when Rahul made a surprise visit to a press meet organised by <a href="/wiki/Ajay_Maken" title="Ajay Maken">Ajay Maken</a> on the topic<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-83"><a href="#cite_note-83">[83]</a></sup>. The ordinance, earlier, was cleared by the government to negate a Supreme Court's judgment that would have led to the ejection of convicted lawmakers.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-84"><a href="#cite_note-84">[84]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-85"><a href="#cite_note-85">[85]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Other">Other</span></h3>
<p>Gandhi has pushed for the empowerment of women.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-86"><a href="#cite_note-86">[86]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-87"><a href="#cite_note-87">[87]</a></sup> He backed the <a href="/wiki/Women%27s_Reservation_Bill" title="Women's Reservation Bill">Women's Reservation Bill</a> which would allow 33% reservation of all Lok Sabha and state legislative assembly seats for women. This bill passed the Rajya Sabha on 9 March 2010 but has not yet been voted on by the Lok Sabha as of February 2014.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-88"><a href="#cite_note-88">[88]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-89"><a href="#cite_note-89">[89]</a></sup><sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-90"><a href="#cite_note-90">[90]</a></sup> Gandhi has also backed the repeal of <a href="/wiki/Section_377_of_the_Indian_Penal_Code" title="Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code">Section 377 of the Indian Penal Code</a> and the decriminalisation of homosexuality.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-91"><a href="#cite_note-91">[91]</a></sup></p>
