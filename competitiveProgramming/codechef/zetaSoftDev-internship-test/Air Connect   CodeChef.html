Air Connect Problem Code: AIRCNCT
Submit

Tweet

There is an infinite grid. You are given NN points on it. 2 points are said to be connected if the manhattan distance between them is equal to 1. If 2 points aren’t connected you can connect by air-pathways, i.e, you can take a flight from 1 point to the other. The cost of an air-pathway is the euclidean distance between the 2 points.

If there are 3 points A, B, C. Let’s say point A & B are connected(by land or by air), B & C are connected(by land or by air), then by transitivity, A & C are also connected. You have to connect all points in the same time minimizingminimizing thethe totaltotal costcost.

Input:

  • First line will contain TT, number of testcases. Then the testcases follow.
  • First line of each testcase contains NN.
  • Then NN lines follow where i'th line contains the xx and yy coordinates of the i'th point.

Output:

For each testcase, output in a single line the minimum cost with 6 exactly 6 decimal places.

Constraints

  • 1≤T≤101≤T≤10
  • 1≤N≤5001≤N≤500
  • 1≤x≤1061≤x≤106
  • 1≤y≤1061≤y≤106

Sample Input:

1
3
1 1
2 2
3 2

Sample Output:

1.414214
